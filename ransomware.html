Ransomware Awareness: Break the Chains

What is Ransomware?

Ransomware is malicious software that encrypts files or locks systems, demanding payment for decryption. It spreads via phishing, exploits, or infected downloads. Victims face data loss, downtime, and financial demands.

Types of Ransomware

  • Crypto Ransomware: Encrypts important files (documents, photos, databases) and Asks for payment (usually cryptocurrency) to decrypt files
  • Locker Ransomware: Locks devices without encryption. Files are not encrypted, but you can’t access the system
  • Scareware: Shows fake warnings like “Your system is infected!” .Tricks users into paying for fake fixes Example: Fake antivirus pop-ups
  • Ransomware-as-a-Service (RaaS):Ready-made ransomware tools sold to criminals Even non-technical attackers can launch attacks

Prevention Tips

  • 1.Regularly create backups of important files and store them in a safe location, such as an external hard drive or cloud storage. Keep backups offline or disconnected from the main system so ransomware cannot encrypt them. Why?Ensures recovery without paying.
  • 2.Use anti-virus software and firewalls. It's important to obtain and use advanced anti-virus software and firewalls from reputable companies and continually maintain them through automatic updates. Why?Detects and blocks malware.
  • 3.Keep your devices and software up to date. Install updates ASAP for all of your operating systems and applications. Why?Adds security layer against breaches.
  • 4. Exercise caution when opening your messages. Most ransomware attacks begin with some sort of phishing message. Pay attention to emails you get and be on the lookout for phishing attempts. Why?Most attacks start with phishing.

Ransomware Attack Vectors

Ransomware enters via email attachments, malicious links, drive-by downloads, or network exploits. Advanced types use zero-day vulnerabilities or supply chain attacks.

Common Entry Points

  • Phishing emails with infected attachments.
  • Exploiting unpatched software.
  • Cloud Service Misconfigurations
  • USB drives or infected devices.

Responding to Ransomware

If attacked, isolate affected systems, report to authorities, and restore from backups. Never pay the ransom – it funds criminals and doesn't guarantee recovery.

Response Steps

  • 1.Isolate the system: Immediately isolate infected or suspected devices by disconnecting them from all wired and wireless networks, and in severe cases disable core network connections to stop the ransomware from spreading. Why?Stops lateral movement.
  • 2.Secure Backups: Backups are the key to ransomware recovery, so keep them offline, access-restricted, and thoroughly scanned before restoring to prevent reinfection.
  • 3.Report to FBI or local authorities: Report the incident to law enforcement and relevant authorities promptly, as they can investigate, possibly provide decryption tools
  • 4.identify the variant: the ransomware variant to understand its behavior, aid in containment and removal, and use tools that analyze code or ransom notes for accurate detection.
